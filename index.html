About Me

Hello!

My name is Luke Hulsebus. I am a Korean American who has a love for technology that's almost as deep as my love of
video games haha! I've been using computers since the moment I could even understand what a computer was
computers are kind of a family tradition. My first experience with a computer was my dad's old home PC that he used to
let me play Flash games on. I played those games until my fingers hurt and I couldn't keep my eyes open! That love of
old Flash games led me to love a little indie game called, Minecraft. I was enthralled with that game for the longest
time. I made meaningful relationships, and strong friendships, and developed even stronger skills. You see, I wanted to
play this game with the friends I'd made, I wanted to so badly in fact, that I started to use the internet to see IF it
was even possible. It was.

I started to read and learn about servers, RAM, CPUs, and Cores! I had no idea what those things were at the time and I
didn't ever expect it to lead where I am today. I started to put together small pieces and I celebrated every time I got
something to work or found out something new about a piece I'd already made work. I made my first batch script, learned
what Java was, wrote my first cfg file, and even more. Months and months would go by, the friends I made were still here,
and we were still playing that same game we had grown up together on. Despite having a good system to play games with all
of them, I desired to learn more and to improve what we had. So I built my first computer, I booted my first computer from
the BIOS, I accessed my home router through a browser for the first time, I port forwarded for the first time, I wrote the
same cfg file and batch scripts I'd written time and time again. I made my first-ever home server.

And the rest from there was history. I joined the Robotics teams at my local middle school and then high school; I
started to learn about computer support and best practices; I learned (the hard way sometimes) about the importance of
cyber security and strong security protocols in general. Between all of this, I always found time for the people around me,
and for my other non-digital passions! I was in the Boy Scouts for 7 years; I participated in my high school drum line where
I played the bass drum; I played on a travel soccer team for 10 years; I boxed and practiced martial arts for 14 years; and I
tried every sport under the sun. But I always came back to my computer. Every night, after every major life event, after every
low point, I always came back to the game that started it all on the machine that made each time logging on so memorable.

After a long time of moving around, finding myself in people that I never dreamed I'd meet, learning skills I never thought
I'd use it again, I ended up back here again. At my computer, with the same old friends playing the same old game. And to be honest,
I hope I never stop.
